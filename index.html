Sleep Breathing Variability as a Potential Biomarker for SUDEP Risk

Presenting Author · American Epilepsy Society Annual Meeting

Investigated whether breathing variability during sleep could serve as a premortem biomarker for Sudden Unexpected Death in Epilepsy (SUDEP). Analyzed multi-modal EEG and cardiorespiratory recordings from 24 SUDEP patients and matched controls.

Methods Developed custom MATLAB code to extract breathing biomarkers (CV, RMSSD, HF/LF ratio, approximate entropy) from thoraco-abdominal band recordings during NREM sleep. Used Wilcoxon rank sum tests for non-parametric comparison.
Key Finding Coefficient of variation (CV) was significantly higher in the SUDEP group (P=0.0227), suggesting breathing variability as a viable premortem biomarker for risk stratification.
Impact Findings support early identification of at-risk patients and could help prioritize preventive strategies for epilepsy patients.
